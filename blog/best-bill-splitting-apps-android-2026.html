Best Bill Splitting Apps for Android in 2026

📅 8 April 2026 ⏱️ 7 min read ✍️ SplitEven Team

There are two kinds of bill-splitting apps: ones that track who owes what over time, and ones that help you split a specific bill right now. Most people need both at some point — but they solve different problems.

We tested the most popular options on Android in 2026. Here's an honest breakdown of what each one does well, and who it's best suited for.

What to Look for in a Bill Splitting App

  • Speed — how quickly can you split a bill at the table before everyone gets restless?
  • Accuracy — does it handle tax and tip correctly? Can it read a receipt?
  • Multi-currency — important if you travel internationally
  • No account required — some apps require everyone to sign up, which creates friction
  • Privacy — where does your financial data go?

The 5 Best Bill Splitting Apps for Android

⚡

SplitEven

Best for: Restaurant bills & receipt scanning

SplitEven is built around one core idea: scan a receipt, split it immediately. The AI reads every item on the bill, assigns tax and tip proportionally, and lets you share each person's breakdown instantly. It's the fastest way to go from "bill arrives" to "everyone knows what they owe."

Unlike debt-tracking apps, SplitEven focuses on the moment of splitting — making it ideal for restaurants, cafes, takeaways, and supermarket runs. Multi-currency support makes it equally useful abroad.

✓ Strengths
AI receipt scanning, proportional tax & tip, multi-currency, no account needed for splitting, receipt history saved
✗ Limitations
Not designed for long-term debt tracking between friends over months
💸

Splitwise

Best for: Long-term expense tracking between friends

Splitwise is the most established name in the space and excels at tracking ongoing balances. Add an expense, split it, and Splitwise remembers who owes what indefinitely. It's excellent for groups that share many expenses over time — roommates, long-term travel partners, couples.

✓ Strengths
Long-term balance tracking, groups, simplifies debts, widely used
✗ Limitations
No receipt scanning, manual entry for every expense, premium features behind paywall
✈️

Tricount

Best for: Group trips

Tricount is specifically designed for group travel. Create a trip, add all expenses as you go, and Tricount works out the optimal settlement at the end — minimising the number of transfers needed. Clean, simple interface with no account required.

✓ Strengths
No account required, optimised settlement, clean UI, good for trips
✗ Limitations
No receipt scanning, designed for group projects not one-off meals
📊

Settle Up

Best for: Regular groups with many expenses

Settle Up takes a similar approach to Splitwise but with a cleaner interface and more straightforward pricing. Good for groups that share many recurring expenses — weekly dinners, shared subscriptions, regular outings.

✓ Strengths
Clean design, recurring expenses, multiple currencies, debt simplification
✗ Limitations
No receipt scanning, less widely known so harder to get friends to join
🧾

Tab

Best for: Quick in-the-moment splitting

Tab is a lightweight app focused purely on splitting a single bill quickly. Enter amounts, split them, done. It's simple and fast but lacks the depth of other apps — no receipt scanning, no history, no ongoing tracking.

✓ Strengths
Very simple, fast to use, no setup required
✗ Limitations
No receipt scanning, no history, no multi-currency support

Which App Should You Choose?

It depends on what you're trying to do:

  • Splitting a restaurant bill right now → SplitEven. Fastest, most accurate, handles tax and tip correctly.
  • Tracking who owes what over months → Splitwise. The most mature product for long-term group balances.
  • A group trip where everyone pays different things → Tricount. Designed for exactly this use case.
  • A regular group with many shared expenses → Settle Up. Clean and well-suited to recurring group costs.
  • You just want to split one bill quickly with no setup → Tab for simplicity, or SplitEven if there's a receipt.

Many people end up using two apps: SplitEven for in-the-moment bill splitting, and Splitwise or Tricount for tracking longer-term balances. They serve different moments and complement each other well.
